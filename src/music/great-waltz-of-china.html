Great Waltz of China
for spoken chorus and mixed chamber ensemble

Berklee World Strings and Chorus --  Eugene Friesen, Conductor Yazhi Guo, dizi & suona; Tao Guo, erhu

Great Waltz of China is LeVines’ arrangement and orchestration of composer Mark Simos’ pentatonic ‘fiddle tune’, with Chinese and English texts by composer Rujing Huang. The unusual scoring is for two treble speaking choruses, three traditional Chinese winds - dizi (flute), suona (oboe-horn), zhonghu (spike fiddle), and string orchestra. Compositional desing elements are strongly influenced by China’s traditional wuxing, translated as the five phases or elements.

The poetry begins and ends in dream time ... a vision of a woodcut ... Along the Great Wall of China are musicians in fanciful mixed garb, European formal waistcoats and top hats, beautiful patterned Chinese robes. On both sides are dancers, Western and Eastern mingling in every sort of pair...
— Devachan Music, Mark Simos
